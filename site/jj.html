  1. Telehealth and telemedicine

by Anis Uzzaman

Source: https://www.inc.com/anis-uzzaman/top-business-technology-trends-in-2021

Institutions, especially in health care, are working to lower
the exposure of Covid-19 to patients and workers. Many private
and public practices have started implementing more telehealth
offerings such as doctor-patient video chats, A.I. avatar-based
diagnostics, and no-contact-based medication delivery.

Telehealth visits have surged by 50 percent compared with pre-
pandemic levels. IHS Technology predicted that 70 million
Americans would use telehealth by 2020. Since then, Forrester
Research predicted the number of U.S. virtual care visits will
reach almost a billion early in 2021.

Teladoc Health, Amwell, Livongo Health, One Medical, and Humana
are some of the public companies offering telehealth services to
meet the current needs.Startups are not far behind. Startups
like MDLive, MeMD, iCliniq, K Health, 98point6, Sense.ly,
and Eden Health have also contributed toward meeting the growing
needs in 2020, and will continue offering creative solutions in
2021. Beyond telehealth, in 2021 we can expect to see health
care advancements in biotech and A.I., as well as machine
learning opportunities (example: Suki AI) to support diagnosis,
admin work, and robotic health care.
